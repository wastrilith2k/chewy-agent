## Order prescription items

- Search for the prescription item and add it to your cart.

- At checkout, enter your pet and vet information and choose your prescription approval. We can contact your vet for you, or if you have the original prescription handy, you can mail it to us.

We'll send you updates about your order status and reach out to you if any action is needed. Most customers receive their prescription items within 3 to 5 business days.

## Prescription approval

Chewy Pharmacy can only fill prescriptions written specifically for your pet by a licensed veterinarian.

When you check out, we ask how you want to get vet approval for your prescription item.

If you choose "Chewy contacts vet", we'll work with your vet clinic and reach out to you if any action is needed.

If you have the original prescription, you can mail it to us. Here's how to mail in a prescription.

Getting an approval typically takes 1 day.

## Mailing in a prescription

In some cases, we may need you to send us the original prescription to get approval for your prescription item.

Due to regulations, we can't accept pictures or photocopies of prescriptions.

Write your order number (from your order confirmation email or order details) on the prescription and mail it to:

Chewy Pharmacy
3621 Fern Valley Rd
Louisville, KY 40219

For gabapentin prescriptions, write your order number on the prescription and mail it to:

Chewy Pharmacy
360 Research Drive
Pittston, PA 18640

## Expired prescription items

### How to tell if a medication has expired
- Check for an expiration date printed by the manufacturer on the bottle or box.
- Check for an expiration date printed on the pharmacy's label.
- Speak with your veterinarian if you have any questions about the safety of the medication. If the validity of any medication is in question for any reason, properly dispose of the item rather than risking the safety of your pet.

### Disposing of expired medication
Many local pharmacies have drug take back programs and will dispose of them properly for you. More information on locating drug take back locations and proper disposal of unused medicines can be found on the following FDA website.

Please call Chewy Pharmacy at 1-877-977-3879 or chat with us, anytime to get more information.

## Veterinary diet authorization

Chewy can only sell veterinary diets which have been specifically authorized for your pet by a licensed veterinarian.

When you check out with a vet diet item in your order, we'll ask for your pet and vet clinic details so we can contact your vet for authorization. We'll reach out to you if any action is needed. Getting authorization typically takes 1 to 2 days.

If you have a copy of the vet diet authorization handy, you can email it to us at vetdiet@chewy.com or upload an authorization to your order details.

## Transfer a prescription

If your pet already has a prescription at another retail or online pharmacy, you can transfer it to Chewy. Our pharmacy team will contact your current pharmacy and handle the process for you.

### Start a transfer
To request a transfer, contact Chewy Pharmacy and share a few details:

Chat with us or call 1-877-977-3879.
- Provide your Chewy account information.
- Share your pet's name.
- Give the name and phone number of your current pharmacy.
- Provide the prescription number.
- Let us know if you're transferring one or multiple prescriptions.

Once we have this information, we'll reach out to your current pharmacy to begin the transfer.

### What to expect
After your request is submitted, we'll work directly with your current pharmacy to complete the transfer. Timing can vary depending on how quickly we receive the required information.

We'll reach out if anything else is needed to complete your request.

### Next steps
Start your transfer by chatting with our pharmacy team or calling 1-877-977-3879. If you have questions along the way, we're here to help.
